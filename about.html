University History

Founded in 1876

Our university was established in 1876 with a vision to provide accessible education to all. What began as a small college with just 35 students and 7 faculty members has grown into a world-renowned institution with over 40,000 students and 2,500 faculty members across multiple campuses.

The original campus was built on donated land from the Westfield family, with the iconic Westfield Hall being the first building constructed. This historic building still stands today as a testament to our enduring legacy.

Mission and Vision

Our Mission

To advance knowledge through innovative research and scholarship, to transform lives through exceptional teaching and learning experiences, and to serve society by fostering an inclusive environment where diverse perspectives thrive. We are committed to creating opportunities for intellectual growth, creative expression, and personal development while promoting ethical leadership and social responsibility. Through our dedication to academic excellence and community engagement, we aim to empower individuals to become thoughtful, informed citizens who contribute meaningfully to their local and global communities.

Our Vision

To be a global leader in education, research, and innovation that addresses the world's most pressing challenges and prepares students to lead with integrity in an interconnected world. We envision a future where our institution serves as a catalyst for positive change, fostering breakthrough discoveries, nurturing creative talent, and developing solutions to complex global issues. Through interdisciplinary collaboration, technological advancement, and cultural exchange, we strive to create an educational environment that inspires excellence, promotes sustainability, and cultivates leadership for generations to come.

Campus Locations

Our university has a global presence with campuses across 3 continents, providing students with diverse learning environments and international perspectives. Each campus offers unique academic programs while maintaining our commitment to excellence.

Main Campus

Westfield, California, USA

Our flagship campus spans 500 acres with state-of-the-art facilities, historic buildings, and beautiful green spaces. Home to all undergraduate programs and most graduate schools.

Administration

Dr. Jonathan Blackwell

President

Dr. Blackwell has served as President since 2018. With over 30 years of experience in higher education leadership, he previously held positions as Dean of the College of Arts and Sciences and Vice President for Academic Affairs. He holds a Ph.D. in Economics from Harvard University.

Under his leadership, the university has expanded its global presence, strengthened research initiatives, and enhanced student success programs. Dr. Blackwell is committed to advancing the university's mission of excellence in education, research, and service.
